Rhodopsalta - Redtail Cicadas

Rhodopsalta has three recognised species.
These cicadas prefer a life around water, usually around the coast.
An interesting fact about Rhodopsalta is their lack of alarm call, a trait shared with the Maoricicada genus.
They are rather small, and have a kind of lanky figure that makes them a bit easier to identify from the other genera.

Closer Look

This genus has no bends in its forewing[1] costa[2] . The pterostigma[3] is not transparent in this genus, and the basal membranes [4] connecting the forewings to the body are usually red or orange. The R crossveins are not coloured in this genus.[5]
The segments of this genera's abdomen have a black and red-orange dorsal pattern. The collar of this genera's pronotum[6] does not laterally extend outward. [7] This genus also sometimes has the vertical yellow line on their pronotum like Amphipsalta. The body of this genus is usually less than 19mm.

Species Descriptions

Rhodopsalta cruentata - Blood Redtail Cicada

Rhodopsalta cruentata is the most widespread member of the Rhodopsalta genus.
The costa of this species' forewing is a gradient of majorly green, progressing into a dark red, with the veins green or yellowish-green. The wingspan can reach 41-44mm. [9]
The body is bright reddish-orange-brown, with symmetrical black patterns on its body. Along their bodies, there is a centred, silver vertical line from the head to the abdomen, the line varies in completeness and width. The abdomens on this species are black, with red-orange lines along the margins. The body length of Rhodopsalta cruentata reaches over 14mm. The mesosternum [10] of Rhodopsalta cruentata is nearly completely white. [11]
This species prefers trees and shrubs, dune grass, and beech forests. It can be found in coastal, lower altitude, lowland and subalpine environments. [11]

Colour Morphs

Rhodopsalta cruentata is majorly brown, orange or red in colour. The colour of this species depends on which location it resides in. The complex eyes are usually brown or amber. The black patterning seems to vary from similar to Amphipsalta, where the orange/red/brown on its body takes majority, to something closer to Kikihia, where the black takes majority, allowing the silver stripe along its back to be strikingly visible.
On the forewing costa, the green can be bright or washed-out olive. This species also has a gold-winged morph.

Distribution

These cicadas can be found all throughout the North island, and the northern, upper western, and coastal regions in the South Island. [9] [13] [14]

Rhodopsalta leptomera - Sand Dune Redtail Cicada

Rhodopsalta leptomera has a body length of around 16-23mm, [11] and are usually a pale orange, or a darker orange than Rhodopsalta cruentata, or dark red. Their bodies also have black patterning, and can also be mostly black in colour with few pale spots. Like others of its genus, there is a pale, vertical silver stripe along its body. Though, in this species, the line can be invisible on the head and/or thorax, or barely visible at all.
They are the hairiest of the genus, the body covered in silvery hairs, making them appear pale, and covering up body markings.
This species' costa and veins can be red-orange, or yellow-orange. It has a wingspan of 43-46mm. [11]
The black abdomen has faintly red margins, but this is usually obscured by the hairs on its body.
This species prefers lowland and coastal areas. They enjoy being on sand dune vegetation near water.

Colour Morphs

This species seems to be quite varied. The main colouration is usually pale, with darker colours visible on closer inspection, but some are a very bright orange.
The complex eyes can be black, pale brown, amber, grey, or olive green.
There is something to be said about the pale colour morph's similarity to the Australian "sand fairy."
A rare morph has been found with bright blue eyes. [15]

Distribution

These cicadas are endemic to small areas in the North Island, around the western coast mainly, and possibly accidentally introduced in a small area at Castle Point. [9] [13] [14]

Rhodopsalta microdora - Little Redtail Cicada

Rhodopsalta microdora is the smallest of its genus, with a body length of 14mm. [9] The species can come in pale green, yellow-green, or reddish, with black markings that vary in size like its sibling species. Like Rhodopsalta cruentata, the mesosternum is almost entirely pale. There is a long, vertical, silver stripe along its body. The stripe is almost always visible on the head, but with varied visability on the thorax, and entirely absent or barely visible on the abdomen.
A distinction from Rhodopsalta cruentata is that green Rhodopsalta microdora individuals have green legs. [14]
The abdomen has dark orange or reddish margins.
The wingspan of Rhodopsalta microdora reaches up to 34-37mm, [11] and the forewing costa, like Rhodopsalta cruentata, is mostly a green colour, but instead fades into a orange-brown, and is much paler.
This species prefers both low- and inland coastal environments.

Colour Morphs

This species also seems to be varied in colouration. It's likely many observations of this species are incorrectly identified, or due to the lack of research into the Rhodopsalta genus and its members, could be split into another subspecies. However, this would require genetic testing. The current idea seems to be that there are two genetic groups, southern and northern.
similarly to Rhodopsalta cruentata, this species has a gold-winged morph.
